The End of All Things

Page Five

I put the puppy back under the bush, too scared to take him with me. I hope he will be okay on his own. I continue travelling north and make good time. I don't run into anyone at all on my way. The forest lies ahead.

As I enter the forest I can barely contian my excitement. I move closer to the cabin and worry that I won't find my family there. All the fears I have chosen not to think about, come to the surface. What if they went somewhere else? What if they didn't make it out? What if they got turned?

I see the cabin through the trees right up ahead.
